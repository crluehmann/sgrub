I am a recent graduate with a B.A. in Psychology and English from the University of Alabama. I minored in the Blount Undergraduate Initiative, a cross-disciplinary honors program that allowed me to capitalize on the extensive resources of a large university while absorbing a well-rounded liberal arts education. My primary focus in my intellectual pursuits has concentrated on attention to detail within the inner workings of the human mind, critical thinking about the influences of perspective, and the connections bridged by language.

 

Modest in its conception, Personally Yours began with my harvesting the miscellaneous disposals of furniture from the traffic of college students in Tuscaloosa for the purposes of up-cycling. In my own meandering experience as a student of behavior and art, I have found the values and ethos within both disciplines to have an undeniable reciprocity. Scavenging the city streets for salvageable wood furnishings became a season. Repurposing, retouching, and refinishing developed into a lifestyle of recognizing the beauty—the potential—of the mundane, the discarded, and the arbitrary. Rooted in a town with community upholding a sturdy local backbone, I derived a novel sense fulfillment crafting custom furniture—the fruit of my labor—in world overwhelmed by mass production. What once served as my rewarding pastime evolved into a business intended to bring a warm personal touch to your home.

 

The first set of wood furnishings I up-cycled became the conglomerate unofficially referred to as the Intimations of Imagination collection. The collection’s name stems from a nod to William Wordsworth’s “Ode: Intimations of Immortality” in addition to the indicated influences of Van Gogh, Klimt, Monet, and other greats fueling my inspiration and frequenting my pieces. Initially feeling too bashful to envision myself as the artist—the creator—I opted to synthesize my efforts as the artist and the critic to be an interpreter in my venture by offering my own artistic license to the breathtaking works I sought to recreate. Regarding the role of the critic in relation to the artist, Oscar Wilde reasoned, “The critic will certainly be an interpreter […] and will look upon Art as a goddess whose mystery it is his province to intensify, and whose majesty his privilege to make more marvelous,” and it has been a privilege to fashion this artful practice.

 

The interpreter lends fresh eyes and innovative methods of communication to offer insight on the ramifications of another’s happenings. It is my hope that my study of this role of interpreting will be as comprehensive as it is a transforming personal endeavor.

 

Thank you for your interest and for your consideration! I am very grateful to have your business.
