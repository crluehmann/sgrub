My Foundation

Modest in its conception, Personally Yours began with my harvesting the miscellaneous disposals of

furniture from the traffic of college students in Tuscaloosa for the purposes of up-cycling. Rooted in a town with community upholding a sturdy local backbone, I derived a novel sense fulfillment

crafting custom furniture—the fruit of my labor—in a world overwhelmed by mass production. What

once served as my rewarding pastime evolved into a business intended to bring a warm personal touch

to your home.

 

 

In my own meandering experience as a student of behavior and art, I have found the values and ethos

within both disciplines to have an undeniable reciprocity. Scavenging the city streets for salvageable

wood furnishings became a season. Repurposing, retouching, and refinishing developed into a lifestyle

of recognizing the beauty—the potential—of the mundane, the discarded, and the arbitrary.

 

 

The first set of wood furnishings I up-cycled became the conglomerate unofficially referred to as the

Intimations of Imagination collection. The collection’s name stems from a nod to William Wordsworth’s

“Ode: Intimations of Immortality” in addition to the indicated influences of Van Gogh, Klimt, Monet, and

other greats fueling my inspiration and frequenting my pieces.
